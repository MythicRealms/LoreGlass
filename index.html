LoreGlass

Every book is a door. Every choice writes a life.

A persistent narrative RPG engine where characters travel between authored worlds, carrying everything they've earned, and everything they've lost.

⚠ A note to travelers

Some doors lead to death. Your character is a cumulative record of every world they have walked. If they fall, that record is erased. Authors are obligated to mark high-lethality books. Choose your doors wisely.

The Golden Rule
Core Vision
"Authors aren't just writing stories. They are writing a character's life."

LoreGlass is a persistent narrative ecosystem. Every book is a door, not a vacuum. The engine is neutral. It does not enforce fairness. It is the author's moral obligation to balance challenge, label lethality, and respect the player's investment. The UI is a literal lens: a piece of glass through which you experience a database of living history.

For Players

For Players

Your story. Your character. Your history.

LoreGlass plays like a living book. Each adventure is an authored world you enter through the engine's lens: prose, choices, and consequences. A full RPG system runs silently underneath. Your character is not a save file. They are a record.

📚
The Library Hub
The central nexus where characters are born and return. A safe space between worlds, and a potential gameplay space in its own right. Every door in the Library is a book an author has built for you to enter.
⚔
A Real RPG System
Six core stats. Seven equipment slots. Feats, backgrounds, scrolls, and a tactical combat engine with initiative and status effects. Your build matters from the first character screen through every world you enter.
📦
Inventory That Travels
Every item is a unique instance, snapshotted at acquisition. Carry a weapon earned in one world into another. Its stats, lore, and history travel with it regardless of whether the new book has ever heard of it.
🧠
The Rereading Mechanic
Memory keywords persist across runs. If your character has been here before, they remember. The story changes. Authors use this to unlock paths only veterans can access: skip the search, rally the village, take the door you earned last time.
🎲
Dice With Real Stakes
Stat checks with actual consequences. Roll 1d10 + your stat against a difficulty. The engine does not soften failure. Both outcomes branch the story forward. Some failures are permanent. Some doors don't reopen.
🌐
Cross-Book Travel
Your character is not bound to one story. Travel between books carrying inventory, feats, memories, and persistent flags. Authors can link their worlds together, or send you back to the Library to choose your next door.
Memory System
The Rereading Mechanic
Memory keywords are permanent chronicle entries. They survive New Runs, book exits, and character loading. Authors use them to detect returning players and unlock branches that only someone who has already lived through an event could access.
Example: Memory / ChildFound
First Visit "Search the surrounding area for the missing child. You have no leads."
Memory Unlocked / Returning Traveler "I know where they take them. Rally the others. We move before dawn."
For Authors

For Authors

Write worlds. Not code.

The engine is a database reader. Content is separated from the machine. LoreGlass ships with a full companion authoring tool. Every card, quest, item, and enemy is defined in plain JSON, but never by hand. Built for mental flow: fast context-switching, live audit, zero dead ends.

01
Context-Switching Canvas
Six tabs per card: Canvas, Choices, Conditions, Effects, Encounter, Library. Auto-saves on card switch. Jump to any linked card with one click. The Recent tab tracks your last 20 cards so you never lose your thread in a complex decision tree.
02
Full Item, Race & Background Libraries
Define weapons, armour, scrolls, and consumables in a structured editor. Type-driven UI shows only relevant fields. Define your world's races with per-stat spinboxes. Override engine defaults or add entirely new races to character creation.
03
16 Conditions, 26 Effects
Every choice checks conditions (keyword, stat, quest state, inventory, story memory) and fires effects that reshape the world. give_item, set_memory_kw, teach_feat, apply_status, and more. Complex branching with zero scripting required.
04
Combat, Merchants & Bounties
Any card triggers full multi-round combat with named enemies and status-on-hit effects. Merchant cards get structured buy/sell row editors. Bounty boards pull from fixed quest rows or generated daily jobs. The engine handles all dice math.
05
Lethality Transparency
Authors have a moral obligation to the player's time. If a book is a "Character Killer," label it as such. The engine provides permadeath and resurrection tokens. How you use them defines your book's contract with the player.
06
Project Audit
Scan the entire book for broken links, orphaned cards, keywords set but never read, or read but never set. Covers all 16 condition types, all effect types, and item gives. Ship clean stories with confidence. No broken doors.
07
Cross-Book & Library Links
Any choice can travel to another book, return via _prev_book, or exit cleanly to the Library with _library, for chapter breaks, story endings, and world-spanning design. Start Over vs Resume is always the player's choice, never the author's.
08
Feats, Quests & Backgrounds
Define feats with triggers: passive, active, on-hit, low HP, combat start. Build story quests with full tracking: active, complete, failed. Create backgrounds with stat bonuses and starting gear. Character creation auto-populates from your JSON.

The System

Under the Hood

A full RPG in every book.

LoreGlass doesn't simulate an RPG. It runs one. Every stat, slot, status effect, and keyword bucket is live state. Authors choose how much of the system to surface in their world.

⚔
Combat Engine
Initiative rolls, 1d10 attacks, DR reduction, status-on-hit, flee and permadeath
🛡
7 Equipment Slots
Main, off-hand, head, body, legs, feet, back. All tracked on the character.
📦
Instance Inventory
Every item is a unique snapshot. Stats and lore travel between books intact.
🧪
Scrolls & Consumables
heal_hp, cure_status, apply_status. Usable from the Items tab at any time.
💫
Status Effects
Poisoned, burning, bleeding, regenerating, stunned, cursed. Tick every card.
🗝
4 Keyword Buckets
Book, quest, story (survives New Run), memory (permanent chronicle)
🎲
Stat Rolls
1d10 + any of 6 stats vs DC. Success and fail branches, optional modifier.
💰
Economy
Silver integers, dynamic markets, bounty boards, merchant buy/sell per town
What's Next

Approaching 1.0

The road forward.

LoreGlass is approaching its first stable release. These are the systems in active development and planned for the 1.0 milestone and beyond.

v0.996 / Next
Main Menu Refocus
The menu is functional but impersonal. Character summary on the main screen showing race, level, last book, and current gold. A proper Continue state that shows where the character left off. The Library redesigned to feel like choosing a world. Lethality labels displayed before entering a book so players know what they are stepping into.
v0.997 / Combat
Combat Finalization
Current combat resolves too fast with no visual weight. Pacing between rounds, hit and miss feedback, status indicators during the fight. Data architecture for multi-enemy encounters established now so it does not require a rewrite later. Initiative tracking, turn order, targeting, and per-enemy effect stacks designed here even if the full multi-combatant UI ships post-1.0.
v0.998 / Polish
Final Polish
First-launch onboarding for new players. Error handling for missing or corrupt book files. Save file versioning so future engine updates can migrate old saves. Audio polish, font size setting, a proper credits screen, and book version checking so players see a clear warning if a book requires a newer engine version.
v1.0 / Release
First Release
First chapter bundled and shipped. Content manifest live on GitHub. All four platforms tested: PC, Mac, Android, HTML5. Character creation, the Library, cross-book travel, and the full item and combat systems stable and documented for community authors.
Post-1.0 / Engine
Customizable Tracking Systems
Authors define entirely new tracking modules: ship inventory, crew morale, trade routes, city revenue, GP/day tracking for owned properties, or any system their world requires. Define it in JSON and the engine reads it. No engine modification needed.
Post-1.0 / Combat
Multi-Enemy and Party Combat
Full multi-combatant encounters with initiative tracking, individual turn order, per-enemy targeting, and area effects. Groundwork for a party system where authored companions join the player across books carrying their own stats and history.
Post-1.0 / Author
Author Companion Preview Mode
A read-only render mode inside the Author Companion showing exactly what a card looks like to a player. Prose rendered, choice buttons with conditions evaluated against a test character, item previews. Reduces back-and-forth between authoring and playtesting.
Post-1.0 / Themes
Theme Creator and World Skinning
A full theme creator for authors to define the UI appearance of their specific book. Custom colours, backgrounds, fonts, and editor themes per world. Authors define the look of their door, not just the words inside it.
Post-1.0 / Safety
Character Backups and Permadeath Toggle
Local and optional cloud character backups so a single bad encounter does not erase years of history. An opt-in permadeath disable toggle negotiated between the player and the book's lethality label. High-lethality remains the default. Safety nets are opt-in.
Post-1.0 / The Library
The Library as a Living Space
Right now the Library is a menu. The long-term vision is an authored location your character walks into between adventures. A librarian who remembers your history. A trophy room. A notice board. The engine already supports this. The hook will exist before 1.0 so no rewrite is needed when the time comes.
Development Chronicle

Development Chronicle

From first page to engine.

LoreGlass began as a single Godot 4 scene. This is an honest record of how it became something else entirely.

v0.1 / The First Spark
Proof of Concept
  • Single-scene Godot 4 prototype. Hardcoded cards and choices.
  • First JSON card format sketched out
  • The pivot: from code-based stories to a JSON-reading database
v0.2 / The Book System
Dynamic Card Loading
  • JSON-driven card loading from book folders on disk
  • Library screen and character selection
  • First persistent keyword system. Story flags that outlive sessions.
  • Save/load via per-character .dat files
v0.3 / Characters Live
Character Creation & Stats
  • Full 3-step character creation: name, race, background
  • Six core stats: Might, Finesse, Grit, Cunning, Charisma, Lore
  • Race presets with stat arrays and starting gold
  • HUD tabs: Stats, Items, Equipment
v0.4 / Swords and Wounds
Combat Engine
  • Turn-based multi-round combat with initiative rolls
  • Attack resolution: 1d10 + Finesse vs AC
  • DR damage reduction; status effects: poisoned, burning, bleeding, stunned, frozen
  • Flee, victory, and permadeath defeat navigation
v0.5 / The Market
Economy & Merchants
  • Merchant card type with structured buy/sell rows
  • Currency stored as silver integers. 100s = 1g.
  • Dynamic market pricing with per-town multipliers
  • Merchant funds, stock tracking, cargo encumbrance
v0.6 / Notice Board
Quests & Bounties
  • Bounty board card type with generated daily jobs per town
  • Fixed story quest rows with accept, complete, and fail keywords
  • Quest progress and merchant turn-in
  • Active quest reminder in Stats HUD
v0.7 / Between Worlds
Cross-Book Travel
  • goto_book link type for cross-book navigation
  • Breadcrumb system: _prev_book and _prev_card magic values
  • Transition overlay with custom title text
  • _library destination: exits cleanly to the Library, character intact.
  • User book folder (user://Books/) shadows and extends built-ins
v0.8 / The Author's Desk
Author Companion App
  • Full companion authoring tool: Canvas, Choices, Conditions, Effects, Encounter, Library tabs.
  • Directory + Recent card explorer; start_card pinned and highlighted
  • Structured editors for combat, merchant, and bounty board cards
  • All 16 condition and 26 effect types with full tooltips
  • Project Audit: broken links, orphaned cards, keyword index
  • Auto-save on card switch; jump-to-card → button on every choice row
v0.9 / Feats and Lore
Feats, Backgrounds, Stat Rolls
  • Feat system: passive, active, on-hit, low-HP, and combat-start triggers
  • Background library with stat bonuses and starting keywords
  • Stat roll choice type. 1d10 + stat vs DC, success/fail branch.
  • Resurrection tokens and on-death intercept
  • Typewriter prose effect with configurable speed and skip
  • Eight UI themes. Applied instantly, saved to settings.
v0.95 / The Living World
Quest System & Memory Keywords
  • quest_keywords dictionary, separate from book and persistent keywords.
  • Quest registry with metadata keyed by keyword ID
  • Quests tab: active, complete, failed. All rendered with status icons.
  • Story keywords (survive New Run) and memory keywords (permanent chronicle)
  • Memories HUD tab: Learned section and Chronicle section with descriptions

v0.95.5 / The Item Library
items.json & Structured Items
  • items.json as the central item registry: weapons, armour, consumables, trade goods.
  • Seven slot fields: main, off, head, body, legs, feet, back
  • use_effect structured editor: heal_hp, apply_status, cure_status
  • on_equip / on_unequip effect rows per item

v0.95.8 / The Item Engine
Instance Inventory & Full HUD Rebuild
