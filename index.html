For Players

For Players

Your story. Your character. Your choices.

LoreGlass plays like a living book. Each adventure is an authored world you explore through prose, choices, and consequences — with a complete RPG system running silently underneath every page.

📖
Books You Can Lose Yourself In
Each LoreGlass book is a handcrafted story world — prose, atmosphere, characters with memory. Not menus and loading screens. Your character grows across books, carrying scars, coin, and history between worlds.
⚔
A Real RPG System
Six core stats. A full equipment system with seven slots. Feats, backgrounds, scrolls, and a tactical combat engine with initiative, weapon procs, and status effects. Your build matters from character creation through every book you enter.
🧪
Inventory That Travels
Every item is a unique instance — weapon, armour, scroll, or trade good — snapshotted at acquisition. Carry a healing draught from one world into another. The blade you earned in a war story fights just as well in a fantasy kingdom.
📜
A Character Who Remembers
Persistent keywords, story memories, and chronicle entries survive New Runs and cross-book travel. A Memory tab tracks what your character has learned, witnessed, and earned — across every world they've visited.
🎲
Dice With Real Stakes
Stat checks with actual consequences. Roll 1d10 + your stat against a difficulty. Both success and failure branch the story forward — no dead ends, only different paths through the consequence of your roll.
🌐
Cross-Book Travel
Your character is not bound to one story. Travel between books carrying inventory, feats, memories, and persistent flags. Books can link directly to each other — or send you back to the Library to choose your next world.
For Authors

For Authors

Write worlds. Not code.

LoreGlass ships with a full companion authoring app. Every card, quest, item, and enemy lives in plain JSON — but you never write a line by hand. The Author Companion turns your vision into a living game.

01
Card-Based Story Authoring
Write prose, set choices, chain cards across six tabs — Canvas, Choices, Conditions, Effects, Encounter, Library. Auto-saves on card switch. Jump to any linked card with one click. Audit the whole book for broken links and missing conditions.
02
Full Item Library
Define weapons, armour, scrolls, consumables, trade goods and quest items in a structured editor. Type-driven UI shows only the fields that matter — combat stats for gear, use_effect rows for consumables, on_equip triggers for both.
03
Races & Backgrounds
Define your world's playable races with per-stat spinboxes, HP, starting gold, description, and advantages summary. Create backgrounds with stat bonuses, starting keywords, and gold modifiers. Character creation auto-populates from your JSON.
04
Conditions & Effects
Every choice checks conditions — keyword, stat, quest state, inventory, story memory — and fires effects that reshape the world. 16 condition types. 26 effect types. give_item, set_story_kw, set_memory_kw, teach_feat, and more. No scripting required.
05
Combat & Merchant Cards
Any card can trigger full multi-round combat with named enemies, custom stats, and status-on-hit effects. Merchant cards get structured buy/sell row editors. The engine handles all dice math, initiative, DR, and market pricing.
06
Feats, Quests & Bounties
Define feats with triggers (passive, active, on-hit, low HP, combat start). Build story quests with full tracking — active, complete, failed. Bounty boards pull from fixed quest rows or generated daily jobs per town.
07
Cross-Book & Library Links
Any choice can travel to another book, return to a previous world via _prev_book, or exit to the Library with _library — for story endings, chapter breaks, and world-spanning narrative design. All from a simple popup.
08
Project Audit
Scan the entire book for broken card links, orphaned cards, and keywords set but never read — or read but never set. Now tracks story_kw, memory_kw, has_item conditions and give_item effects. Ship clean stories with confidence.

The System

Under the Hood

A full RPG in every book.

LoreGlass doesn't simulate an RPG — it runs one. Every stat, every item slot, every status effect is live state. Authors choose how much of it to surface.

⚔
Combat
Initiative rolls, d10 attacks, DR reduction, multi-enemy, status-on-hit
🛡
7 Equipment Slots
Main, off-hand, head, body, legs, feet, back — all tracked on the character
📦
Instance Inventory
Every item is a unique snapshot — stats and lore travel between books intact
🧪
Scrolls & Consumables
heal_hp, cure_status, apply_status, modify_vars — use from Items tab anytime
💫
Status Effects
Poisoned, burning, bleeding, regenerating, stunned, cursed — tick every card
🗝
4 Keyword Buckets
Book, quest, story (survives New Run), and memory (permanent chronicle)
🎲
Stat Rolls
1d10 + any stat vs DC — success and fail branches, optional bonus modifier
💰
Economy
Silver integers, dynamic markets, bounty boards, merchant buy/sell per town
Development Chronicle

Development Chronicle

From First Page to Engine

LoreGlass began as a single Godot 4 scene. What follows is an honest record of how it became something else entirely.

v0.1 — The First Spark
Proof of Concept
  • Single-scene Godot 4 prototype — hardcoded cards, hardcoded choices
  • Prose label, choice buttons, the most basic loop proven
  • First JSON card format sketched out
v0.2 — The Book System
Dynamic Card Loading
  • JSON-driven card loading from book folders on disk
  • Library screen and character selection screen
  • First persistent keyword system — story flags that outlive sessions
  • Save/load via per-character .dat files
v0.3 — Characters Live
Character Creation & Stats
  • Full 3-step character creation: name, race, background
  • Six core stats: Might, Finesse, Grit, Cunning, Charisma, Lore
  • Race presets with full stat arrays and starting gold
  • HUD tabs: Stats, Items, Equipment
v0.4 — Swords and Wounds
Combat Engine
  • Turn-based multi-round combat with initiative rolls
  • Attack resolution: 1d10 + Finesse vs AC, player and enemy
  • DR damage reduction system
  • Status effects: poisoned, burning, bleeding, stunned, frozen
  • Flee, victory, and permadeath defeat navigation
v0.5 — The Market
Economy & Merchants
  • Merchant card type with structured buy/sell rows
  • Currency stored as silver integers — 100s = 1g
  • Dynamic market pricing with per-town multipliers
  • Merchant funds, stock tracking, and cargo encumbrance
v0.6 — Notice Board
Quests & Bounties
  • Bounty board card type with generated daily jobs per town
  • Fixed story quest rows with accept, complete, and fail keywords
  • Quest progress bar and turn-in at merchant cards
  • Active quest reminder shown in the Stats HUD
v0.7 — Between Worlds
Cross-Book Travel
  • goto_book link type for cross-book navigation
  • Breadcrumb system: _prev_book and _prev_card magic values
  • Transition overlay with custom title text
  • not_installed_label for books the player doesn't have yet
  • User book folder (user://Books/) shadows and extends built-ins
  • _library destination — ends a book cleanly and returns player to Library
v0.8 — The Author's Desk
Author Companion App
  • Full companion authoring tool (LoreGlassAuthor.gd)
  • Six tabs: Canvas, Choices, Conditions, Effects, Encounter, Library
  • Directory + Recent card explorer, start_card pinned and highlighted
  • Structured editors for combat, merchant, and bounty board cards
  • Condition and effect dropdowns with full tooltips
  • Project Audit: broken links, orphaned cards, keyword index
  • Auto-save on card switch with notification; jump-to-card → button
v0.9 — Feats & Lore
Feats, Backgrounds, Stat Rolls
  • Feat system with passive, active, on-hit, low-HP, and combat-start triggers
  • Background library with stat bonuses and starting keywords
  • Stat roll choice type — 1d10 + stat vs DC with success/fail branch
  • Resurrection tokens and on-death intercept
  • Typewriter prose effect with skip button and configurable speed
  • UI themes — eight presets, applied instantly, saved to settings
v0.95 — The Living World
Quest System & Story Flags
  • quest_keywords dictionary — separate from book and persistent keywords
  • Quest registry with metadata keyed by keyword ID
  • Quests tab: active, complete, failed — all rendered with status icons
  • Condition types: quest_active, quest_complete, quest_failed, quest_not_active
  • Story keywords (survive New Run) and memory keywords (permanent chronicle)
  • Memories HUD tab: Learned section and Chronicle section with descriptions
v0.95.5 — The Item Library
items.json & Structured Items
  • items.json as the central item registry — weapons, armour, consumables, trade goods
  • Slot fields: main, off, head, body, legs, feet, back
  • Lore field — item descriptions shown inline in Equip tab expand panel
  • use_effect structured editor in Author Companion — heal_hp, apply_status, cure_status
  • on_equip / on_unequip effect rows per item
  • feats.json, backgrounds.json, and races.json editors added to Library tab
  • Book-defined races load on top of engine built-ins (same id = override, new id = added)
v0.95.8 — Checkpoint ★ Current
Instance Inventory & Full HUD Rebuild
  • Instance-based inventory — every item is a unique snapshot with its own iid
  • Stackable vs unique: trade goods use qty, gear gets one record per physical item
  • Item snapshots travel cross-book — a sword from one world works in another with zero definition overlap
  • Direct sell at 20% of base value from Items tab; equipped items blocked until unequipped
  • Scrolls (type: scroll) shown on Main HUD as emoji+qty; consumables in Items tab only
  • heal_hp, apply_status, cure_status wired into apply_effect — potions and scrolls fully functional
  • Regeneration status ticks every card transition — both "regeneration" and "regenerating" status names supported
  • has_item, has_story_kw, has_memory_kw, not_story_kw as live condition types
  • give_item and remove_item as card and choice effect types
  • unload_character() — clean slate on delete, create, and main menu return; no state bleed between characters
  • Legacy potions integer removed from Main HUD; all consumables now live in unified inventory
  • Items HUD: Weapons, Armour, Consumables, Scrolls, Trade Goods, Commodities, Quest Items, Other
  • Author Companion: type-driven item row visibility, stackable checkbox, races tab, save buttons synchronous
  • Audit expanded to track all 16 condition types and all effect types that set or read state
